# ANIMA Character Creator — Product Specification

## 1. Product Overview

**ANIMA Character Creator** is a fantasy web app for players in the ANIMA RPG campaign. The app introduces players to the continent of Lemuria, guides them through ANIMA-specific character choices, and generates a polished printable A4 PDF character sheet at the end.

The experience should feel like opening an ancient fantasy dossier, magical parchment folio, or campaign archive. It is not a full live character tracker. It is a guided character creation and printable sheet generator.

### Core product promise

Players can:

1. Learn about Lemuria and its major mysteries.
2. Create a level 1 ANIMA character step by step.
3. Choose ANIMA-specific race, origin, faith, class, background, equipment, and story details.
4. Upload an optional character portrait.
5. Export a beautiful A4 printable PDF character sheet.

### Scope principle

The app focuses on world-specific character identity and printable output. It does not need accounts, online saving, multiplayer, admin tools, or a live gameplay sheet.

---

## 2. Target Users

### Primary users

Players in the ANIMA campaign who need help understanding the world and creating a playable level 1 character.

### Secondary users

Portfolio viewers who should understand the project as a polished UI/UX and product design piece.

---

## 3. Product Goals

* Introduce players to the world of Lemuria without overwhelming them.
* Make ANIMA-specific choices feel exciting, visual, and meaningful.
* Use a guided wizard structure so non-expert players can follow along.
* Track race/origin/class/background choices and selected proficiencies.
* Leave most mechanical math blank so players can fill and update by hand.
* Produce a visually impressive A4 PDF sheet that feels custom to the chosen race.
* Keep the implementation simple enough for a low-code/coding-agent build.

---

## 4. Key Product Decisions

* Web app.
* No login.
* No account system.
* No database required.
* No saving required after page refresh.
* Hardcoded content.
* Guided step-by-step flow.
* A4 PDF export.
* Character name is mandatory.
* Most fields are optional and can be left blank for handwriting later.
* No automatic stat/math calculations.
* No subclass selection in the app.
* PDF includes an empty Subclass / Path / Order field.
* Spells are not selected in the app.
* Spell sheet page is included only for spellcasting classes.
* Equipment is flavor-only and does not affect stats.
* The app should use original ANIMA text, custom icons, and custom art direction.

---

## 5. Global UX Structure

### Step count

The character creation flow has **9 steps after the Welcome screen**:

1. Learn About the World
2. Choose Race
3. Choose Origin / Ancestry
4. Choose Faith
5. Choose Class
6. Choose Background
7. Choose Equipment
8. Character Details & Portrait
9. Review & Export

### Global navigation

Every screen after the Welcome screen should include:

* Top-left back icon.
* Decorative diamond progress indicator.
* Current step title.

Suggested progress indicator style:

```text
◆ ◆ ◆ ◇ ◇ ◇ ◇ ◇ ◇
```

Filled diamonds represent completed/current progress. Empty diamonds represent remaining steps.

### CTA behavior

* Long exploratory screens may use a sticky bottom CTA.
* Short choice screens use a normal bottom CTA below the content.
* Required screens hide or disable Continue until valid.
* Optional screens always allow Continue.

### Required vs optional card behavior

Required card-selection screens:

* Used for Race, Origin / Ancestry, Class.
* No selection by default.
* Tapping a card selects it.
* Tapping another card changes selection.
* Tapping the selected card again does not deselect it.
* Continue appears only after required choices are complete.

Optional card-selection screens:

* Used for Faith, Background, Equipment.
* No selection by default.
* Continue is always available.
* Tapping a card selects it.
* Tapping another card changes selection.
* Tapping the selected card again deselects it.

---

## 6. Visual Art Direction

The app should feel like an ancient fantasy dossier, magical parchment folio, or in-world character archive.

### Overall style

* Fantasy parchment UI.
* Warm cream, sand, gold, sepia, ink brown, and muted accent colors.
* Elegant serif typography for headings.
* Readable body typography.
* Simple symbolic icons.
* Decorative but restrained borders.
* Magic, astrology, relic, sun, moon, star, and folio-inspired ornaments.
* Large illustrations for major identity choices.
* Smaller custom icons for compact choice cards.

### UI principles

* The UI should feel magical and tactile, not like a generic form.
* Cards should feel collectible and parchment-like.
* Ornament should frame content, not overwhelm it.
* Body text must remain readable.
* The PDF is the visual hero artifact.

### Cards

Major cards use:

* Large illustration.
* Title.
* Short flavor line.
* Optional mechanic summary.
* Parchment border.
* Clear selected state.
* Race cards may include a selected-state animation: the default card shows a still image, and when selected, the image area plays a one-time animation that ends on its final frame.

Compact cards use:

* Custom icon.
* Title.
* One-line description.
* Optional Recommended badge.
* Selected state.

### Icons

Use custom ANIMA icons, not copied official D&D icons. Icons should be simple, readable, and thematic.

# Screen 1 — Learn About the World

## General intro text

Lemuria is a small and ancient continent, separated from the wider world by dangerous seas, old storms, and stranger things still. Sailors tell stories of reefs that move in the night, of whales that sing in forgotten languages, and of lights beneath the waves where no city should be. Those who reach Lemuria find a land of deep forests, golden deserts, shattered islands, silent ruins, and mountains ruled by dragons.

But Lemuria is best known for its curse.

Every human-like creature born on the continent carries the touch of this curse. Slowly, across generations, bodies change. Ears sharpen, eyes gleam in the dark, skin becomes scale, feather, fur, or fin. Some people remain stable for centuries as proud peoples with their own cultures and kingdoms. Others change further. In the worst cases, memory, language, and selfhood fade, until nothing remains but instinct.

Some call this fate a curse.
Some call it nature’s will.
Some call it the punishment of forgotten gods.

Whatever the truth, every people of Lemuria lives in its shadow.

The oldest stories say there was once a great civilization before the Bashet, before the Nald, before the Wildling tribes, before even the oldest cities now standing. The ruins of that civilization are scattered across Lemuria: broken pillars in the desert, drowned halls beneath the sea, stone roads swallowed by roots, and towers cut into the mountains. No one knows what these people called themselves. Most simply call them the Ancients.

Their language is mostly lost. Their cities are dead. Their purpose is unknown.

And yet their shadow remains everywhere.

## Lore content structure

### The Curse

The Curse does not strike all people in the same way. For some, it is barely more than ancestry: a stable form passed from parent to child. For others, it is a slow, frightening transformation. A person may live a normal life and yet watch their grandchildren grow more beastlike than they were. A family may remember a time when their ancestors walked on two feet and spoke clearly, only to now guard an elder who can no longer form words.

Among scholars, priests, and village storytellers, the stages of the curse are often described in different ways. In Rem, learned Bashet speak of bloodlines and inherited forms. In Atlantis, Nald priests say the Deep protects them from the worst of the curse. In the Wilds, druids say the body only follows the soul’s true shape. Among the Drakanis, many believe dragon blood places them beyond the curse entirely.

None fully agree.

Most people, however, understand one thing: the curse is not finished.

The fear is not only that people have changed. The fear is that one day, over generations, all culture may be lost. Cities may empty. Songs may become howls. Names may become meaningless. The peoples of Lemuria may become nothing but animals wandering among the ruins of their own kingdoms.

This is why adventurers, scholars, priests, and desperate families still search for a cure.

Many believe the source of the curse lies somewhere in the Silent Land, the dead territory ruled by the Silent Queen. Others believe the dragons know more than they admit. Some whisper that the Nald’s Ancients of the Deep are not merely gods or protectors, but keepers of a terrible truth.

* No card selected by default.

## Default helper text

**Click on a card to learn more about each people of Lemuria.**

## Race cards

Race cards may support selected-state animation. Each race card should have a default still image and may have an optional animated selected-state asset provided by the designer. When the player selects a race card, the animation plays once and stops on its final frame. It should not loop. Only the selected card should animate. When another race is selected, the previous card returns to its still or final non-active visual state and the newly selected card plays its own one-time animation. If an animated asset is missing, the card should fall back to the still image.

Respect reduced motion preferences: if reduced motion is enabled, do not play the animation and show the still or final frame instead.

## Drakanis breath weapon rule

At level 1, a Drakanis can release the elemental power of their dragon blood.

* Uses: once per long rest.
* Save DC: 8 + Constitution modifier + proficiency bonus.
* Damage at level 1: 1d10.
* Successful save: half damage.

PDF should print the rule as reminder text, not calculate the DC.

## Purpose

Let players optionally choose a faith or worldview. Faith affects background suggestions, roleplay flavor, and the PDF identity section. It gives no mechanics.

## Layout

* Intro text.
* Five cards.
* Detail panel.

## Intro text

Faith shapes many lives in Lemuria, but not every character is devout. You may choose a belief, leave it uncertain, or reject faith entirely.

## Cards

1. Khazar and the Thirteen Muses.
2. The Ancients of the Deep.
3. Dragon Gods and Dragonlords.
4. Elementals and Spirits of the Wilds.
5. No Religion / Undecided.

## Optional behavior

* No faith is selected by default.
* Tapping a selected faith again deselects it.
* If no card is selected, PDF faith field stays blank or says Not chosen depending on layout needs.
* If No Religion / Undecided is selected, PDF says **None / Undecided**.

## Faith descriptions

### Khazar and the Thirteen Muses

The faith of Rem tells of Khazar, a legendary hero beloved by thirteen divine Muses. Their stories are full of romance, beauty, wine, art, jealousy, tragedy, pleasure, and courage.

The Bashet of Rem do not see joy as shallow. To them, music, love, dance, poetry, feasting, and desire are sacred acts of remembrance. A person who celebrates life defies forgetting.

### The Ancients of the Deep

The Nald worship the Ancients of the Deep, mysterious protectors said to dwell beneath Atlantis. They are believed to guard the Nald from the curse and preserve their culture beneath the waves.

The priesthood teaches that the Deep remembers what the land forgets.

Few outside the highest ranks of the Nald faith know what lies beneath the temples of Atlantis.

### Dragon Gods and Dragonlords

The Drakanis honor ancient dragons, some as gods, some as ancestors, some as living rulers. Among them, faith and politics are often inseparable. To serve a Dragonlord may be a religious duty, a family bond, a military oath, or a cruel necessity.

Stories of the oldest dragons suggest they were present before the modern peoples of Lemuria arose. Whether they are guardians, tyrants, witnesses, or cowards depends on whom one asks.

### Elementals and Spirits of the Wilds

The Wildlings honor the spirits of nature: elementals, animal guardians, rivers, storms, stones, roots, fires, and the great cycle of change. Their faith has no single temple or scripture. It lives in practice.

A mask may be a prayer.
A hunt may be a hymn.
A migration may be a holy text written across the land.

To the Wildlings, the world is alive, and every living thing is becoming something.

### No Religion / Undecided

Your beliefs are private, uncertain, conflicted, or your own.

## Class descriptions and tags

### Fighter

Disciplined, versatile, and relentless. Fighters are trained warriors found among city guards, mercenary companies, noble houses, caravan escorts, Dragonlord armies, and rebel bands. In ANIMA, a Fighter is someone who has turned survival into craft.

Class skill proficiencies: Choose 2.

Often found among: All peoples, especially Drakanis and Bashet.

### Barbarian

Rage given form. Barbarians draw power from fury, instinct, grief, spirit, bloodline, or the raw violence of the changing world. Many come from the Wilds, borderlands, deserts, warbands, or communities where civilization’s laws have little reach.

Class skill proficiencies: Choose 2.

Often found among: Wildlings, Bashet Sandstriders, Ruby or Emerald Drakanis.
